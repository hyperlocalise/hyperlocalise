---
title: "What is hyperlocalise?"
description: "CLI-first localization workflows for draft generation, curation sync, and status reporting."
---

`hyperlocalise` helps you generate local translation drafts, sync with your TMS, and track what still needs review.

## Who this is for

Use this CLI if you:

- keep translation files in your repository,
- want AI-generated drafts as a starting point,
- still need human curation in POEditor, Lokalise, or Crowdin.

## Core workflow

| Stage | Action | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | [`init`](/commands/init) | Scaffold `i18n.jsonc` and bootstrap defaults. |
| 2 | Configure [`i18n config`](/configuration/i18n-config) | Define locales, buckets, and LLM/storage settings. |
| 3 | [`run --dry-run`](/commands/run) | Validate plan and detect issues before writing drafts. |
| 4 | [`run`](/commands/run) | Generate local draft translations. |
| 5 | [`sync push`](/commands/sync-push) | Upload local changes to your TMS. |
| 6 | Curate in TMS | Human review and correction in your translation platform. |
| 7 | [`sync pull`](/commands/sync-pull) | Bring curated translations back into the repository. |
| 8 | [`status`](/commands/status) | Measure completion and unresolved work. |
